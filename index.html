Public traffic problem

Traffic lights should understand waves, not just count cars.

Every red light is not just a timer problem. Drivers hesitate, queues travel backward, and vehicles arrive in uneven waves. Static signal plans miss that physics, so cities lose time, fuel, and clean air.

Open sector replay See the model
Goal Predict the next wave and recommend safer green windows before live control.
Problem

Green time is wasted when the next link is already blocked.

A signal can turn green and still fail the corridor if the downstream queue has nowhere to go. PATC focuses on this sector-level failure, not one isolated junction.

Why now

Replay makes safer experimentation possible.

The first version runs in shadow mode: it watches, predicts, and compares recommendations against fixed-time and actuated baselines before asking anyone to change a live signal.

Public benefit

What people should feel if this works.

01 Fewer avoidable stops

Less stop-start motion across linked signals, especially where one blocked link causes a cascade.

02 Less idle waste

Lower delay should reduce fuel burn and emissions once field baselines prove the effect.

03 Operator trust

Every recommendation needs a reason, confidence score, fallback, and replay trace.

Safety boundary

No black-box live control.

PATC starts as a replay and recommendation layer. Live control only makes sense after operator review, pedestrian protection, minimum greens, emergency priority, stale-data stops, and manual override.
