Problem

One green can make the next junction worse.

In corridors like HSR, Silk Board, and Bommanahalli, the failure is often coordination. A signal releases traffic into a downstream link that is already blocked. Vehicles stop again, the queue moves backward, and the next signal inherits a worse problem.

PATC treats traffic like flow: density, discharge, startup loss, spillback, weather, and event waves. The first product is not an uncontrolled AI switch. It is a measured replay system that proves when an adaptive action helps and when it should fall back.

Product

A decision layer for connected signal sectors.

01

Observe

Temporary cameras, field notes, phase logs, weather, events, and manual labels create the first trusted sector dataset.

02

Estimate

Traffic-wave math and AI measurement estimate queues, discharge, startup loss, and downstream blocking risk.

03

Replay

The model compares fixed-time behavior with candidate timing choices before recommending anything operational.

04

Recommend

Operators see an action, reason, confidence score, safety constraints, and a replay trace for audit.

Public benefit

The city benefit is not a faster demo. It is less wasted life at red lights.

For commuters

Fewer unnecessary stops, shorter queue cascades, and less time burned in avoidable signal delay.

For traffic police

Replay-backed recommendations reduce manual guesswork and make each timing change explainable.

For cities

Existing infrastructure gets a better coordination layer before expensive civil works are considered.

Safety boundary

No black-box live control.

Year one is observe, calibrate, replay, and shadow-test. Minimum greens, pedestrian windows, emergency priority, stale-data stops, downstream blockage checks, privacy limits, and manual override are hard constraints.
