The platform

A decision layer for traffic signals that know when they do not know.

Most traffic-signal upgrades begin with better detection. PATC begins with safer decisions. Detection is only one input; the platform also models queue pressure, downstream blockage, confidence, and fallback behavior.

ObserveTraffic phase, queue growth, approach demand, weather/noise context
EstimateUncertainty, confidence, discharge rate, spillback risk
SimulateCompare timing options against the current signal plan
RecommendExplainable green-time action with human override and fallback rules

Why Bengaluru first

A hard first testbed for adaptive signal decisions.

Bengaluru is not the whole market. It is the stress test. Dense junctions, irregular arrivals, rain, glare, lane indiscipline, blocked turns, and downstream spillback make it a strong proving ground for adaptive traffic intelligence.

TomTom's reported 2025 morning rush-hour time for 10 km in Bengaluru.

Deployment path

Built to earn trust before control.

  1. Shadow modeWatch recorded or simulated signal plans first; use live observation only after authority-approved access.
  2. Operator dashboardShow the queue estimate, confidence, reason, expected effect, and fallback condition.
  3. Mock controllerProve phase logic on safe hardware before any road-side integration.
  4. Pilot protocolMove toward municipal pilots only after the system produces a repeatable validation report.
