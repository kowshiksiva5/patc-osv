Technical engine

The mechanical intuition is the product edge.

Traffic behaves like constrained flow: density builds, discharge drops near saturation, and one blocked link sends pressure backward. PATC combines that physical view with probabilistic startup and rolling recommendation.

Use the triangular fundamental diagram correctly: free-flow rises to capacity, then congested flow falls toward jam density.

Bengaluru pilot path

Replay-only until permission, proof, and safety gates exist.

  • Candidate sector: 4-6 connected signals with visible upstream/downstream queue interaction.
  • Data boundary: observation, replay, and dashboard recommendations only; no controller writes.
  • Validation matrix: peak/off-peak, weekday/weekend, rain/no-rain, school/holiday, event/anomaly, and seasonal variation.
  • Handoff: baseline comparison, fallback rules, operator notes, and pilot-readiness memo.

Company

Two childhood friends turned a daily traffic wound into a flow problem.

Kowshik Motepalli and Sidvik Basa grew up seeing the same Bengaluru traffic failures, then started asking why one cleared signal still leaves the next road locked. Kowshik brings AI/software depth: simulation, data workflow, decision logic, and product interface. Sidvik brings mechanical engineering judgment: flow through bottlenecks, discharge, validation, testing discipline, and the instinct to measure before claiming.

Together, they are turning lived commute pain, field observation, simulation, and flow intuition into shadow-mode intelligence for mixed-traffic city sectors. If it works in Bengaluru, the same sector model can travel to any city where queues interact across junctions.
