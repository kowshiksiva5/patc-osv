What PATC proves first

A corridor cockpit for multiple signals, not a single-light tool.

The first product is not autonomous signal control. It is a shadow-mode dashboard that watches a sector, estimates queue pressure across connected roads, simulates timing and offset choices, explains the recommendation, and records a validation trail.

The platform

A signal layer that treats traffic like flow through constrained channels.

Most traffic-signal upgrades begin with better detection. PATC begins with safer coordination. Detection is only one input; the platform also models density, discharge rates, queue-wave growth, downstream blockage, confidence, and fallback behavior.

ObserveSignal phase, queue growth, approach demand, seasonal and weather context
Model flowDensity, discharge, conservation, queue waves, and spillback risk
CoordinateCompare timing and offset options across connected signals
RecommendExplainable action with confidence, human override, and fallback rules

Why Bengaluru first

A hard first testbed for sector-level signal coordination.

Bengaluru is not the whole market. It is the stress test. Dense roads, irregular arrivals, rain, glare, lane indiscipline, blocked turns, and downstream spillback make it a strong proving ground for coordinating multiple signals under uncertainty.

Deployment path

Built to earn trust before control.

  1. Shadow modeWatch recorded or simulated sector traffic first; use live observation only after authority-approved access.
  2. Operator dashboardShow queue waves, confidence, reason, expected effect, and fallback condition.
  3. Mock controllerProve timing and offset logic on safe hardware before any road-side integration.
  4. Pilot protocolMove toward municipal pilots only after seasonal tests produce a repeatable validation report.
