Behind the traffic math

The model turns queues into pressure, uncertainty, and safe actions.

The homepage stays public-facing. This page holds the deeper model: traffic wave physics, stochastic arrivals, startup loss, optimization, auditability, and the references behind the terms.

Core model

From traffic waves to green-time decisions.

01

Conservation of vehicles

rho(t+1) = rho(t) + dt / dx * (q_in - q_out)

A road link is treated like a density field. If more vehicles enter than leave, pressure rises. If discharge clears the link, pressure falls.

02

Flow curve

q = Q(rho)

Flow rises in free traffic, peaks near critical density, then falls as the link approaches jam density.

03

Startup loss

q_eff(t) = s(t) * Q(rho)

A green signal does not create instant motion. Drivers hesitate and discharge recovers over time after red.

04

Probabilistic arrival

arrival_rate = lambda(t)

Demand changes with rain, school release, events, and peak waves. PATC should estimate uncertainty instead of assuming one fixed flow.

Controller

Optimize pressure, but never ignore safety.

min E[sum pressure + spillback_penalty + safety_penalty]

In replay, the controller scores alternative green windows using queue pressure, downstream blocking risk, movement recovery, and hard constraints. The action is a recommendation until field validation and operator approval exist.

Estimator Kalman or particle filter

Estimate density, arrivals, discharge, and turning movement uncertainty from sparse sensors.

Optimizer MPC or constrained policy search

Score the next few signal windows while respecting minimum greens and spillback limits.

Audit Counterfactual replay

Store state, action, predicted effect, confidence, and rejection reason for every recommendation.

Term notes

Words used in the simulation.

DemandVehicle arrival rate into a link or sector.
DischargeVehicle outflow when a movement is served by green.
Safety biasA conservative offset that prefers lower-risk actions under uncertainty.
Peak spillbackA queue grows backward and blocks upstream links.
Event surgeA short, non-stationary arrival wave from concerts, schools, offices, or festivals.
